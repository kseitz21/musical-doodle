Focus on the Music

Chamber Music International Non-profit Website Redesign

Task

Redesign a non-profit website.

Role

UI/UX Designer (group project)

Time

3 weeks

Discover

Starting our initial assessment of the website, a couple big things stood out right away: information overload and readability issues. During testing, users were struggling to find the information needed to purchase tickets since it was spread out all across the website. Even though the information was on the site, the overall design of the website was preventing users from getting the information they needed.

Define

As we started our brainstorming process, we knew that our best plan of attack was to start the website design from scratch. While we came up with a lot of feasible ideas, we wanted to make sure each area of concern was addressed while also balancing our capabilities within the short timeframe. Our feature prioritization matrix seen below helped us make sure to focus our energy on the features that would be the highest impact in the lowest time period first, then expand from there as time allowed.

We also broke up our feature list into categories and put them in order of highest priority within the category. That gave us a clear guideline of the things we wanted to accomplish as a group and allowed for easy reference back to during the design process.

  • Visual Components
    • Color Scheme for accessibility and visibility
    • Organization of home screen hierarchy
  • Navigation Components
    • Reorganize submenu options/add additional as needed
    • Connecting concert information to payment information for smoother use
  • Content Components
    • Reduce information overload on home page (esp. side columns)
    • Organize information together that needs to be together
    • Making sure sponsoring organizations are clearly visible
    • Adding audio/video clips to support content as needed

Develop

With feature priority in mind, we turned our focus into redesigning the layout of the home page. We wanted to find a happy medium between creating a design that was familiar to older uses, but also fresh to bring in new users. We went back and forth between a couple different designs-a trendy bento box style or a more basic but classic layout.

We were torn between the two designs since they served different purposes that would both technically solve some of our problems, so we ultimately looked back at our research and decided that we should go with the bento box, as it allowed us to still present a high amount of content all while keeping it condensed and clean, plus it kept all the information in a smaller distance with minimal scrolling. We iterated on that design with a few adjustments that continued to emphasize the hierarchy of the information presented.

Deliver

Finally feeling confident in our layout decisions, applying the UI design elements felt much smoother. While continuing our efforts to keep elements of the original website familiar to returning users, we knew that changes would have to be made to the original color scheme of the website. The original background and text color did not pass accessibility with only a 2.6 rating. So, we darkened up the background and used the old background color as an accent color to keep the color present on the website, plus added a few other pops of color to help draw attention when needed.

The bento box layout allows for a lot of information to be added, but also keeps it visually contained to help readers assess what information is needed. In the future, we’d want to add additional elements to the website to further help users navigate and use the website. We want to expand on the payment system and have it more embedded into the website to make sure users don’t have to leave the website to complete their purchase.
